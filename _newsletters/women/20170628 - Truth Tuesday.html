Fellowship Women,

#truthtuesday has been such a wonderful time so far! Here are your weekly encouraging words from Amanda Mae, our Class Administrator.

Ladies,

Sometimes we’re afraid to take the next step after we’ve been hurt. It’s hard to trust God when you’re in a season of ashes, there aren’t many clear answers, and the “clues” like Devon shared in her hiking story don’t seem like they’re enough to keep you on the right path.

Like Devon wanted her friend Jill to “just lead” because Jill was the author of the clues and knew exactly where the trail was, I often want God to just tell me what to do. But most of the time He just stays with me and gives me the space to discover what it is He has for me. I stumble upon the small blooms and think “this has to be it”, until I turn the corner and discover the giant pink rhododendrons right around the corner. WHOA—what sweet joy is in store for us when we truly experience His great love! It’s the love that transforms our hearts and allows us to pour out His love and grace onto others.

Are your expectations of God too small?

Are there any circumstances or areas of your life that you have put up walls to keep from experiencing Gods abounding love?

Take heart, friends: Though God may call us to sit in the ashes for a time, it is so we can see His work and experiences His love. Surrender with both arms raised and allow Abba’s abounding love to hold you.

Love,

Amanda Mae Steele

Class Administrator
